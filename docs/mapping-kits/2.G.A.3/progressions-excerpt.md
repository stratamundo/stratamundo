## Verbatim excerpt

This Progression cites 2.G.3 as background to fractions work:

> In Grades 1 and 2, students use fraction language to describe partitions of shapes into equal shares. 2.G.3 In Grade 3, they start to develop a more general concept of fraction, building on the idea of partitioning a whole into equal parts and expressing the number of parts symbolically, using fraction notation. (p. 8)

Sidebar at the same point cites the standard verbatim:

> 2.G.3 Partition circles and rectangles into two, three, or four equal shares, describe the shares using the words halves, thirds, half of, a third of, etc., and describe the whole as two halves, three thirds, four fourths. Recognize that equal shares of identical wholes need not have the same shape. (p. 8)

The Progression earlier notes (p. 4) that:

> Fraction language and subordinate units. First and second graders use fraction language to describe partitions of simple shapes into equal shares—halves, fourths, and quarters in Grade 1, extending to thirds in Grade 2.

## Extraction for drafters

**What the kid should be able to do**

- Partition circles and rectangles into 2, 3, or 4 equal shares.
- Use the words *halves, thirds, half of, a third of, four fourths* to describe the shares.
- Recognize that equal shares of identical wholes do not have to look the same (e.g., a square can be split into 4 equal squares OR 4 equal rectangles — both are fourths).

**Common misconceptions / developmental hurdles**

- Not explicitly named in the Fractions Progression. (Treatment is in the K–6 Geometry Progression; consult `fractions-misconceptions.json` for SM's project-internal list.)

**Where this skill sits in the progression**

- Earlier: Grade 1 informal halves/fourths/quarters language on shapes.
- Later: 3.G.A.2 (partition shapes into parts with equal areas; express each part as a unit fraction of the whole), and 3.NF.A.1 (formal fraction notation for 1/b).

**Recommended representations / models named in the Progressions**

- Partitioned circles and rectangles (area-share diagrams).
- Pattern blocks ("A red block is half of a yellow block" — Grade 1; "A blue block is a third of a yellow block" — Grade 2; p. 3).

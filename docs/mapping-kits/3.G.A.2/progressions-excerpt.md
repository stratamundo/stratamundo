# Progressions excerpt — 3.G.A.2

**Source:** Progressions-3-5-Fractions.pdf, extracted pages 3, 8 (cited as bridge from geometric partitioning to fraction notation; main treatment in the K–6 Geometry Progression).

## Verbatim excerpt

> When measuring length in Grade 3, students begin to use rulers with tick marks that indicate halves and fourths of an inch. [...] They are introduced to fraction notation, and their use of fractions and fraction language expands. For instance, when working with pattern blocks, a third grader might use the notation 1/3 in describing a rhombus block as being one-third of a hexagon block. 3.G.2 In the domain of number and operations, use of "half," "fourth," and "third" is extended to include unit fractions, that is, fractions which represent one share of a partition of 1 into equal shares. (p. 3)

Sidebar citing the standard verbatim:

> 3.G.2 Partition shapes into parts with equal areas. Express the area of each part as a unit fraction of the whole. (p. 3, repeated p. 8)

In the context of "equal parts":

> Initially, students can use an intuitive notion of congruence ("same size and same shape" or "matches exactly") to explain why the parts are equal, e.g., when they partition a square into four equal squares or four equal rectangles. 3.G.2 (p. 8)

## Extraction for drafters

**What the kid should be able to do**

- Partition a shape (square, rectangle, circle, etc.) into parts with equal areas.
- Express the area of each part as a unit fraction of the whole (e.g., "each part is 1/4 of the whole square").

**Common misconceptions / developmental hurdles**

- "Equal parts" initially understood as "same shape and same size" (intuitive congruence). The Progressions notes that this can mislead: shaded regions of the same area can look very different and still each be 1/4 of the whole — "even though it is not easily seen as one part in a partition of the square into four parts of the same shape and size" (p. 8). Drafters should expect this rigidity.
- Not otherwise explicitly named in the Fractions Progression; consult `fractions-misconceptions.json` for SM's project-internal list.

**Where this skill sits in the progression**

- Earlier: 2.G.A.3 (partition circles/rectangles into 2, 3, 4 equal shares with words only).
- Later: 3.NF.A.1 (formal fraction notation 1/b and a/b), 3.MD.4 (rulers marked in halves and fourths).

**Recommended representations / models named in the Progressions**

- Pattern blocks (rhombus as 1/3 of hexagon).
- Squares partitioned into equal squares or equal rectangles (area representations).
- Rulers marked in halves and fourths of an inch as a connection to measurement.

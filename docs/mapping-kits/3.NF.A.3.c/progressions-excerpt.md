## Extraction for drafters

**What the kid should be able to do**

- Write a whole number as a fraction with denominator 1 (e.g., 3 = 3/1; 6 = 6/1).
- Recognize fractions like 4/4, 5/5, 8/8 as equivalent to 1.
- Recognize that the whole number 2 has multiple fraction names: 2/1, 4/2, 6/3, 8/4, ...
- Locate whole-number fractions (4/4, 6/3, etc.) on the number line at the appropriate whole-number point.

**Common misconceptions / developmental hurdles**

- Not explicitly named in this section. The Progressions flags this skill as "of particular importance" (p. 9) because it underwrites later work — equivalence to 1 is foundational for subtraction, mixed numbers, and the general equivalence rule. Consult `fractions-misconceptions.json` for SM's project-internal list.

**Where this skill sits in the progression**

- Earlier: 3.NF.A.1 (a/b as a copies of 1/b — so 4/4 is 4 copies of 1/4, which is the whole), 3.NF.A.2 (fractions on the number line).
- Later: 4.NF.B.3.c (add/subtract mixed numbers — relies on writing whole numbers as fractions to regroup), 4.NF.A.1 (general equivalence rule), 5.NF.A.1 (add/subtract with unlike denominators).

**Recommended representations / models named in the Progressions**

- Number line diagrams showing 4/4 located at the same point as 1; 6/3 located at 2; etc.
- The infinite-ruler view of the number line iterated by unit fractions of varying sizes.
